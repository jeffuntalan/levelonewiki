Character Creation Outline

  1. Assign the Standard Array
  2. Choose your race
  3. Choose your background.
  4. Choose your class (don't populate spells when it asks you)
  5. Add spells manually (for spellcasters)

Procedure

1. Navigate to the Actor Directory tab in the top right. 

2. Click on Create Actor.

3. In the popup, enter your character's name (in this case, Ruben).

4. Click on Create New Actor.

5. The empty character sheet should now appear. Go ahead and enter your ability scores (for my players, you can use point buy or standard array)

6. After the ability scores have been entered, click on the three tiny dots beside the close button, just above the character level.

7. Click on Plutonium Import.

8. Race Selection: Click on the Races & Subraces button.

9. Race Selection: Select 5eTools as the source. This will serve as the database for the Races we can select from.

10. Race Selection:Click on the Open Importer button. A list of Races should now appear! Go ahead and select your desired Race.

11. Race Selection: In this guide, I chose Human. It will prompt me to "apply ability score modifications". Go ahead and click Yes.

12. Background Selection: Now that the race is selected, let's move on the the Background. Click on the Backgrounds button in the importer.

13. Background Selection: Once, again, select 5eTools as the source. You get the gist. This will serve as the database of the backgrounds we can select from.

14. Background Selection: Click on Open Importer.

15. Background Selection: You should now see a selection screen similar to the Race selection earlier. Go ahead and select your desired Background. You can use the radio buttons or the search bar at the top.

16. Background Selection: Click on Import. Configure your options. Items, features, and personality traits appear here! You can select the ones you want, don't skip!

17. Class Selection: Okay now for the Class. After your Background has finished importing, click on the Classes & Subclasses button. Once again, select 5eTools as our source. Click on Open Importer and let's configure our class! In this guide, I chose Paladin. When you're done, you can go ahead and Click on Import.

18. Class Selection: Select the character's level when it prompts you. You can choose whatever level you want to start with! Click Ok when you're done.

Optional: You can also select spells in the Class section if you're a caster. I highly recommend not populating your spellbook with all of the spells available since it can be hell to toggle prepared spells later on. You can manually add them later :)

19. We're done! You can now close the Importer and revel at your character sheet. Further edits can be discussed with your DM!
