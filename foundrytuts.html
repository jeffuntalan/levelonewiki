How to join a game in FoundryVTT

You can join Foundry games by going to the link that the host has given you. Links may come in the form of http://ip:port formats (e.g. http://192.168.0.1:3000) which is common in self-hosting setups where the host runs the game through their computer, or it may also come in the form of custom domains (like our very own https://dnd.leveloneadventure.me) which may or may not support SSL. The latter is usually hosted on a dedicated cloud servers, or in some cases, self-hosted dedicated servers.

The Basics

The best way to learn how to play in Foundry is to play a game in it! With enough patience, you could learn the basics of rolling dice, updating your character sheet, moving tokens, and communicating with other players right before a session starts.

Character Creation

Character creation in FoundryVTT can be done in a number of ways: manually, via D&D Beyond Import, or by using a module.

  • Manual character creation is the closest you can get to emulating the pen-and-paper feel of in-person games. While this is still widely practiced, it requires a thorough understanding of the rules of character creation, levelling, and maintenance. This method is compatible with most modules, but it is still possible for it not to play well with automation.
  • D&D Beyond import is by far the simplest method to do. Just send your D&D Beyond link to the DM, and your character, including spells, will be imported in seconds. Updating your D&D Beyond through Foundry is possible, but it is a 3rd party solution that requires supporting the 3rd party's Patreon.
  • Character creation via module is done primarily through Plutonium, a product of 5eTools. It is the closest thing to an automatic character creation tool for now. You can learn more about it here.
